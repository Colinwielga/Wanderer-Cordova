Apocalypse Engine-style moves. A move is simply a fictional and/or mechanical trigger attached to a fictional and/or mechanical outcome. If you do something in the game that counts as an instance of the trigger, the move happens: "If you do it, you do it." If you want to make the move happen, you have to do something in the game that counts as an instance of the trigger: "To do it, do it."

If you're trying to come up with a move but you're stumped for ideas, here are some things that can trigger moves:

  • When your character does something.
  • When your character does something under particular circumstances.
  • When someone else does something to your character.
  • When your character finds themself in a particular situation.
  • When your character's stats are in a particular state.
  • When another move is invoked.
  • Instead of another move that would have been invoked.
  • At the beginning or end of the session.
  • Between sessions or when there's downtime in play.
  • When you spend a point from a reserve.
  • When something important to your character is at stake.
  • When your character uses a thing.
  • From now on.
  • Immediately upon creation and never again.

And here are some effects that moves can cause:

  • Roll dice or draw/discard from the deck.
  • Substitute or change stats.
  • Offer choices from a list.
  • Ask someone to provide particular details.
  • Ask someone to answer a question.
  • Change your character's circumstances directly.
  • Alter the way a different move works.
  • Mark progress towards advancement.
  • Start or advance a countdown.
  • Call for a price to be paid.
  • Hold points to be spent over time.
  • Specify who gets to make a particular decision.
Complex moves might combine multiple options from both lists. Don't hold back. Do what feels right.
